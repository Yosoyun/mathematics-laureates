# Research and Source Policy

## Principle

Accuracy is more important than volume.

## Source Priority

1. Official award organisation
2. Official university or research institution
3. Official personal academic page
4. Mathematical society
5. Peer-reviewed paper or journal
6. Trusted academic database
7. Established reference source

Wikipedia may help discovery but must not be the sole source for important claims.

## Required Source Metadata

Every source should include:

- Title
- Publisher or organisation
- URL
- Source type
- Accessed date
- Optional publication date
- Optional DOI
- Optional archival note

## Fact Categories

Distinguish:

- Official award citation
- Verified biographical fact
- Verified publication data
- Editorial summary
- Simplified explanation
- Advanced explanation
- Incomplete or uncertain information

## Prohibited Fabrication

Never invent:

- Winners
- Award years
- Citations
- Birth or death details
- Nationality
- Institutions
- Advisers
- Collaborators
- Publications
- Prize amounts
- Quotations
- Research contributions
- Mathematical claims
- Image licences

## Prize Values

Prize values may change. Store:

- Amount
- Currency
- Reference year
- Source
- Notes

Do not present old amounts as current.

## Nationality and Affiliation

Keep separate:

- Birthplace
- Citizenship or national affiliation
- Working institution
- Awarding organisation country

Use careful language and avoid collapsing complex identities into one label.

## Quotations

Only include quotations with reliable attribution. Store source and context. Avoid long copyrighted extracts.

## Mathematical Explanations

- Verify definitions and claims.
- Use correct notation.
- Separate intuitive explanation from technical statement.
- Identify prerequisites.
- Avoid implying a theorem says more than it does.
- Prefer original papers, official lectures, and respected surveys.

## Completeness Labels

Every award dataset must use one:

- `verified-complete`
- `substantially-complete`
- `in-progress`
- `requires-verification`

## Verification Date

Every substantive record must include `lastVerified`.

## Corrections

Provide a corrections page and contribution process. Corrections must include a source.
